{% if labels == 'Common-rust' %}

Common rust is caused by the fungus Puccinia sorghi and is the most frequently occurring of the two primary rust diseases of corn in the United States. In years with exceptionally cool summers, high supply yield losses may occur when the leaves at and above the ears become severely diseased. Common rust can be easily distinguished from other diseases such as gray-spot and blight by the development of dark, reddish-brown pustules. To prevent, use corn that is resistant, and not susceptible to the disease. For instance, sweet corn is traditionally more susceptible to this disease than field corn. Also, planting the corn on time early in the season can prevent the disease from infecting the corn.
{% elif labels == 'Blight' %}
Northern corn leaf blight is a disease caused by the fungus Exserohilum turcicum. It is a very common disease that can cause high percentage supply losses. Northern corn leaf blight starts off as gray or bright green spots on the leaf around a quarter to half a month after infection. However, it can continue developing until the leaf exhibits a tan to gray marking up to half a foot long. If still left unnacounted, the disease can spread throughout the whole leaf, destroying the leaf and the corn supply. To prevent corn blight, try planting a resistant variety. You can also plant the corn in an area with plentiful sunlight and not too much moisture, so that the leaf dries quickly after rain.
{% elif labels == 'Gray-spot' %}
Gray spot is a corn disease caused by a fungus called Cercospora zeae-maydis. The fungus thrives in high temperatures and high humidity, but can survive in the winter allowing it to build up over years. Gray spot tends to spread more quickly in the summer and early fall, when corn is harvested. The disease first appears on corn leaves in the form of small, blocky spots that are brown or tan. These spots slowly grow bigger and eventually, the leaf is almost entirely covered in the spots and it dies. Gray Spot can be prevented through hybrid resistance and crop rotation.
